Archiving: Tradition

Buseoksa Temple

A masterpiece of Goryeo Dynasty wooden architecture. It is famous for its "Entasis" (Baeheullim) columns—which swell slightly in the middle to create an optical illusion of stability—and its elegant, honest structural logic.

Byeongsan Seowon

This pavilion exemplifies the principle of "Chagyeong" (Borrowing Views). The open space between the pillars of Mandaeru acts as a series of picture frames, capturing the Nakdong River and mountains to blur the boundary between nature and man-made structure.

Changdeokgung Palace

Unlike rigid, symmetrical layouts, this palace follows the natural topography of the land. It represents the Korean ideal of architecture coexisting with nature rather than dominating it.

Soswaewon Garden

The pinnacle of Korean private gardens (Wonlim). It shows how a scholar's house can be integrated into a valley, using stone walls and streams as part of the architectural experience.

Jeju Traditional House

Unlike mainland houses, Jeju thatched roofs are rounded and tied with ropes in a checkerboard pattern to withstand strong winds. Their low, gentle curves mimic the silhouette of "Oreums" (volcanic cones), creating a unique architectural landscape that dissolves into the horizon.
