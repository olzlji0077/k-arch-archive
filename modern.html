Tradition Modern

Archiving: Modern Influence

Seolhaeone Clubhouse

A contemporary reinterpretation of the "Cheoma" (Eaves). The dynamic, sweeping curves of the traditional roofline are translated into modern geometric panels and materials.

Jeju Podo Hotel

Inspired by the "Oreums" (volcanic cones) of Jeju and the organic rooflines of traditional Jeju thatched houses. It masterfully translates the spatial flow of traditional Korean dwellings into a luxurious, continuous interior experience.

French Embassy in Korea

Designed by a pioneer of Korean modern architecture. It is celebrated for the dramatic, upward-curving concrete rooflines that pay homage to the exaggerated curves of Hanok eaves, blending Le Corbusier's brutalism with Korean lyricism.

Swiss Embassy in Seoul

A modern masterpiece inspired by the Hanok's "C-shaped" (ㄷ-shape) and "Square" (ㅁ-shape) courtyard layouts. It uses a massive wooden structure to recreate the spatial depth and the relationship between the Madang and the surrounding rooms.

GaOnJai

A private residence that reinterprets the traditional "Madang" and "Ru" (elevated pavilion). The jagged, dynamic rooflines and the fragmented volumes create a modern "mountainous" landscape, echoing the spatial sequence of traditional Korean architecture.
